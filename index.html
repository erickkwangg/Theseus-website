AI Virtual Machine (AIVM)

At the heart of Theseus lies the AI Virtual Machine (AIVM), a specialized runtime built to handle data-heavy tasks such as training updates and inference computations. Unlike blockchains that treat AI as an add-on, the AIVM integrates model execution as a fundamental building block.

The AIVM features machine-learning-optimized opcodes that handle AI workloads more efficiently than general-purpose VMs, reducing overhead and boosting throughput. Its scalable architecture accommodates larger models and continuous updates without prohibitive on-chain fees, while enabling AI models to maintain and evolve their states directly on-chain.

Proof-of-Inference

Theseus employs a unique consensus mechanism known as Proof-of-Inference, specifically designed for AI-driven systems. It utilizes a LSH-style validation mechanism, where one inference result can be verified by a validator with 100 times greater efficiency than the initial inference process.

Through this mechanism, Theseus achieves consensus on both deterministic and probabilistic inferences. Model outputs become part of block validation, and if a node incorrectly executes a model or attempts to manipulate its result, other nodes can detect and reject the block proposal. This creates a decentralized safety net that prevents tampering or censorship by any single party.
